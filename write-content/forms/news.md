# Form: News

Something happened externally worth sharing. Not the author's work — something
in the world. Reality-dominant: the external fact is the content.

**Academic grounding:** Kinneavy's referential/informative aim applied to
external events. Britton's transactional function for external communication.

---

## Subtypes

### release — software release or product announcement
A software release, new version, new feature announcement, or book/resource
publication. What changed and why it matters.

**Characteristics:**
- What was released (name, version, key changes)
- Links to release notes, downloads, documentation
- Brief context if non-obvious
- No extended narrative

**Not a release:** a feature showcase that explains how the feature works in
depth → Article/explanation (optionally cross-posted with News/release secondary)

---

### event — conference, talk, meetup
Pre-event announcements, event information shares (slides, videos, agenda),
brief event recaps, boot camp logistics, registration reminders.

**Characteristics:**
- What, when, where
- Who is speaking / what is covered
- How to attend or access the content
- Brief recap for post-event (what happened, slides/video links)

**Not an event post:** a full narrative review of what happened at the event,
with impressions, named people, technical observations →
**Article/commentary** (primary) + News/event (secondary)

**The spectrum:**
| Content | Classification |
|---------|---------------|
| "I'm speaking at JavaOne next week" | News/event |
| "Here are my slides from JavaOne" | News/event |
| "Full house at the session" + brief note | News/event |
| Full narrative: vendors, conversations, insights | Article/commentary + News/event |

---

### industry — observation on the broader landscape
Something happening in the industry worth noting: competitor moves, market
observations, academic papers, standards developments, third-party adoption.

**Characteristics:**
- The external fact or development
- Brief context (why it matters to the reader)
- Opinion clearly separated from fact
- No extended analysis → if analysis is the point, use Article/commentary

---

## What all News posts share

- Reality-dominant — the external event/fact is the content
- Fast and direct — no padding, no elaborate setup
- Clear sourcing — link to the original where relevant
- Opinion separated from fact where both appear
- No summary — ends when the information is given

## Thin posts

A single sentence + a link is a News post. This is acceptable.
Do not inflate a thin news post into an article to make it feel more substantial.
The thinness IS the appropriate response to thin content.

## Cross-posting

News/event + Note/musing: when an event update has a genuine personal voice
component that changes how it should be read.

**Primary determination for News/event cross-posts:**
If you stripped the personal voice, would the event update still work? Yes →
News/event primary. If stripping either element leaves nothing → neither is secondary.

**Example:** "Argentina June Workshop - Volcano update" — informing event
attendees of late arrival (functional purpose) → News/event primary.
Personal amusing tone → Note/musing secondary.

## Length

As short as needed. No minimum. A micro post (20 words + link) is valid.
